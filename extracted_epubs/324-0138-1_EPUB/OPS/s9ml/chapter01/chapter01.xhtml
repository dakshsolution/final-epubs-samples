Ola Parcel: Ola’s EV Parcel Service in India

Case Study

“The use of two-wheeled electric vehicles will disrupt the logistics industry in India, and Ola’s on-demand delivery service will bring about a significant change in intra-city logistics. With its extensive range of services, including ride-hailing, digital commerce, logistics, and electric vehicle manufacturing, Ola is uniquely positioned to implement this transformation quickly and efficiently. We are confident that the collaboration between Ola and Ola Electric will revolutionize the two-wheeler logistics industry in India.”1

– An Ola Representative

On October 7th 2023, the launch of the Ola Parcel service was part of Ola’s commitment towards ’innovative, sustainable, and inexpensive solutions’ which fulfilled the changing customer requirements in the mobility as well as logistics sector. After being launched in Bengaluru, India, the service would be gradually extended to other parts of the country. To draw customers to this new line of service, it was offered to the customers for free on October 7th and 8th 2023.2 The netizens were quite happy with this launch. As explained by a user, “Bengaluru, it’s time to unbox a greener tomorrow with Ola Parcel. Reliable, eco-friendly, and just a ride away! 🏍motorcycle From 2W to 2.0! Ola Parcel’s electric logistics ecosystem is here to power India’s delivery needs. Ready to make the switch?” Another user stated, “Superb. This will give a boost to many small businesses.” “All set to mission pollution free india.. Great,” explained a third user.3

In addition to the Ola Parcel service, Ola had also introduced its electric bike services in Bengaluru to offer accessible as well as affordable everyday commute to the public. This was an important initiative taken by Ola to enhance the ride-hailing experience in the country. It had also introduced other services like Prime Plus, a premium service, in some of the major Indian cities.

As of October 2023, Ola was the biggest mobility platform in India and one of the largest ride-hailing companies across the globe. It provided ‘on-demand transportation services’ to more than one billion people located across three continents. Moreover, Ola also tried to promote sustainable mobility via the production of advanced electric vehicles (EVs) at its Future Factory. The Future Factory was not only the largest but also the most sustainable two-wheeler factory worldwide. Ola’s objective was to transform the world towards sustainable mobility and make a positive environmental impact.4

Ola in India: The Evolution and Growth

Ola was one of the biggest ride-sharing platforms worldwide and was one of the most renowned mobile apps for transportation in India. Previously known as Ola Cabs, the mission of Ola was to create ‘mobility for a billion people’. With this mission in mind, Bhavish Aggarwal (Aggarwal), Co-Founder & CEO, along with Ankit Bhati (Bhati), had started Ola in 2010.5 Both Aggarwal and Bhati were graduates from IIT Bombay.

Prior to Ola, Aggarwal had started an online website called Olatrip.com. It provided holiday packages as well as weekend trips. One day, for his business purpose, Aggarwal had to travel to Bandipur from Bengaluru for which he had opted for a car rental service. However, he had a bitter experience as the driver stopped the car during the journey and asked Aggarwal to negotiate the amount that he would pay to the driver. Since Aggarwal was reluctant to do so, the driver left him on the way before reaching the destination. This made him realise that the trouble that he faced was probably akin to that faced by several customers in India who were looking for quality cab services. This in turn helped him to visualise the scope of a good cab booking service, and hence, he had changed the business model of Olatrip.com to Ola Cabs (Exhibit I and Annexure I).

Ola played the role of a facilitator in offering cab-booking services.6 Rather than buying and then leasing its own vehicles, Ola partnered with the owners and the taxi drivers.7 Customers could book a cab via the Ola app. Ola did not own any cab. Only the drivers who possessed valid permits which were authorised and verified by the transport authorities were eligible to sign up with Ola. These drivers were either self-employed or they worked for an operator who was the owner of several cabs. The drivers could access a ‘driver-specific mobile app’ on their smartphones after registering with Ola. The drivers had the liberty to decide when they wanted to login to the Ola app and accept cab requests for rides from the customers. Ola took a commission of around 15% on all the bookings done via the Ola app. However, there were several factors which were considered by Ola while raising the final bill for the customer. Some of these factors included the base fare, distance fare, peak pricing, etc.8 (Exhibit II).

In September 2023, Ola had also introduced an electric bike taxi service in Bengaluru, thus marking its entry into the electric bike taxi platform. The S1 electric scooters of Ola were used in Bengaluru and they charged ₹25 for a distance of 5 kms and ₹50 for 10 kms. He also shared his plans to roll out this electric bike taxi service in other cities in India.9

Moving ahead, in October 2023, Ola had launched an affordable on-demand parcel delivery service in Bengaluru, India, by using electric two-wheelers, which had placed the company in direct competition with Uber, Swiggy Genie, etc.10 Thereafter, in March 2024, ahead of its IPO, Ola had plans to launch electric autorickshaws, called Raahi, which was the Hindi word for ‘traveller’. The price of Ola Electric was expected to be akin to that of the company’s competitors. Product differentiation was one of the distinguishing factors of Ola which had helped the company to gain competitive advantage over its rivals in the market. It provided some unique services like ‘Ola rentals, Ola city, and Ola outstations’, to target a wide range of customers. The company offered low-cost travel options to the price-sensitive customers, while premium options were offered to those who wanted to pay more to enjoy enhanced services. Also, Ola had made huge investments in technology and innovation. By launching Ola Electric, Ola had positioned itself as an ‘innovative and forward-thinking company’. These marketing efforts of Ola were aimed towards attracting new customers as well as retention of the existing ones.

Ola also relied heavily on social media platforms like Facebook, X, and Instagram for creating brand awareness and facilitating customer acquisition. ‘Creative content, attractive visuals, engaging hashtags, and influencer marketing’ were some of the strategies used by the company to communicate with the target audience.12

On January 25th 2024, Aggarwal highlighted that Ola’s India mobility business had generated a standalone profit of ₹2.5 billion in FY2023 after incurring a loss amounting to ₹0.66 billion in FY2022. In FY2023, the revenue of Ola increased 58% to ₹21.35 billion from ₹13.5 billion in FY2022. Aggarwal explained, “In FY23 we challenged ourselves to not just grow and scale, but to do this profitably. While our revenue continued to grow at a strong clip of 58%, we became EBITDA positive in the India mobility business. This is one of the few consumer internet businesses in India to be profitable at this level. We believe that mobility is largely an underpenetrated category in India currently.” Hemant Bakshi13 , the new CEO of Ola, who had joined the company after remaining over 15 years with Unilever, said that the company’s ‘three pillars of growth in mobility’ included ‘Premiumisation’, ‘Penetration’, and ‘Electrification’. “These would be our focus areas going forward,” he added.14

Ola Launches ’Ola Parcel’ Service: A Bold Business Strategy?

Keeping the three growth pillars in mind, on October 6th 2023, Ola had made an announcement that it had launched an ‘all electric on-demand delivery service’, named Ola Parcel, in Bengaluru (Exhibit III). With this launch, the objective of Ola was to electrify logistic services in India. The service was very affordable, and the delivery solution was accessible to everyone.15 Commenting on this launch, Aggarwal had mentioned in an X post, “Launching Ola Parcel today in Bengaluru! Start of an all electric 2W logistics ecosystem for India!”16 This service was launched by Ola after it had restarted its bike-taxi service in Bengaluru with its electric S1 scooters on September 16th 2023.17

Ola had introduced the Ola Parcel service as part of its commitment to offer ‘innovative, affordable and sustainable solutions’ to fulfil customers’ evolving needs for mobility as well as logistics. After Bengaluru, the service would be gradually extended to other Indian cities. According to an Ola Spokesperson, “2W EVs will disrupt the logistics ecosystem in India and Ola’s on-demand delivery service will bring in a paradigm shift in the intra-city logistics industry. Owing to its robust network of services spanning across ride hailing, digital commerce, logistics and EV manufacturing capabilities, Ola is uniquely placed to bring in this transformation at speed and scale. Ola had also introduced E-bike services in Bengaluru to make every day travel accessible as well as affordable for the general public. This complemented the various initiatives taken by Ola to improve the ride-hailing experience in the country. It had also introduced Prime Plus, its premium service, in cities like ‘Bengaluru, Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune, Kolkata, and Chennai’ to enable the customers to enjoy a smooth and hassle-free journey with professional drivers and to give complete ride assurance, thus doing away with cancellations or operational issues.18

Customers who wished to avail the Ola Parcel service would have to ‘download the Ola app and create an account’. After creating the account, they would have to login and opt for the ‘Parcel’ option from the homepage. Then they would have to enter details pertaining to the ‘pickup and drop-off locations, as well as the weight and dimensions of the parcel’. Once these details were entered by the customer, Ola would provide ‘an estimated fare for the delivery’. The customer would have to confirm the fare and once it was confirmed, a driver would be assigned by Ola for collecting the parcel and delivering it to the drop-off location(Exhibit IV).

Booking Ola Parcel: A Step-by-Step Guide

To book an Ola Parcel, simply follow these steps:

  • Open the Ola app and choose the Ola Parcel option.

  • Enter the destination where you want the item to be delivered.

  • After your driver is confirmed, give them a call to inform them that they’ll be picking up a package.

  • When the driver arrives, share your Start Code with them and place your item in their vehicle.

  • Once the driver reaches the delivery destination, you will receive a call.

Ola Parcel service also came with several features which made the service ‘convenient and user-friendly’. The entry of Ola was the latest entry in India’s ‘parcel pick-up-and-drop service segment’. In this segment, Ola would compete with other ride-hailing firms like Rapido and Uber, and hyperlocal ecommerce platforms like Swiggy Genie and Dunzo backed by Reliance Retail.20 Since 2019, Swiggy Go was an ‘instant delivery service’ which operated in Bengaluru. Later, it was rebranded as Swiggy Genie and was launched in 30 Indian cities in 2020, following the COVID-19 pandemic and the resulting lockdowns that followed. In 2020, Uber had also introduced a similar service known as ‘Connect’ within its app. The service was offered in ‘Gurugram, Guwahati, Jaipur, and Kolkata’.21

However, unlike its competitors, Ola charged nominal rates for its parcel delivery service. For instance, Swiggy Genie charged ₹60 for up to 2 km, then ₹15/km up to 7 kms, and thereafter ₹25/km. Similarly, Rapido, another close rival of Ola Parcel, charged ₹40 for 2 kms, then ₹13/km up to 7 kms, and ₹12/km beyond 7 kms.22 According to Ecosystem Ventures, a venture capital investment and strategic consulting firm, “One of the notable aspects of Ola Parcel is its pricing strategy. With rates starting as low as ₹25 for a 5km delivery, it offers affordability and convenience. Charges incrementally increase for longer distances, with a maximum cap of ₹100 for 20km. This competitive pricing may entice both individual users and businesses looking for cost-effective delivery solutions.”23 Adding to that, market analysts at Buildd24 stated, “This venture puts them against giants like Swiggy Genie, Uber Connect, Porter, and Dunzo. Yet, given Ola’s past, this isn’t just another launch; it’s a bold move. A move signaling disruption.”25 (Exhibit V).

Features Offered by Ola Parcel in Comparison with Rivals

  • Flat Fee Strategy: Ola Parcel has introduced an aggressive pricing model. They’re charging ₹25 for 5km, ₹50 for 10km, ₹75 for 15 km, and ₹100 for 20km. In comparison, Swiggy Genie charges ₹60 for the first 2 km, then ₹15/km up to 7 km. Rapido starts at ₹35 for 2 km, followed by ₹15/km.

  • All Electric Fleet: Ola’s approach isn’t solely about pricing. They’re committing to making all Ola Parcel deliveries using their electric two-wheelers. This initiative not only addresses carbon emissions but also aligns with the global shift towards green transportation.

  • Utilizing Their Own Fleet for Better Economics: By deploying their own electric scooters, Ola will improve their unit economics. This could potentially offer them a competitive advantage in the long run.

  • A Strong Initial Push: To gain traction, Ola is offering the parcel service for free until October 15th. Such initiatives can accelerate user onboarding and gather initial feedback.

Also, another remarkable feature of Ola Parcel was its dependence on electric scooters for deliveries. This move was in tandem with the global trend of shifting to EVs to ensure a more sustainable future. The benefits of electric two-wheelers were that they were eco-friendly as well as cost-effective, thus making them suitable for intra-city deliveries.

However, as highlighted by Ecosystem Ventures, “Ola’s success will depend on its ability to differentiate itself, expand rapidly, and offer a compelling value proposition to users. Further, it needs to be seen if Ola can maintain its pricing and make unit economics work at scale as other players are burning money despite charging more than Ola.”26

In addition, market analysts at Buildd opined, “The delivery market might be crowded, but Ola’s unique offerings, especially their electric fleet and competitive pricing, could differentiate them... However, every new venture has its challenges. Will Ola establish itself in this segment, or will this be another learning? It all depends on maintaining their initial offerings, leasing more scooters to riders, and attracting a growing user base.”27

Against this background, whether Ola could surpass its rivals in the country’s parcel pick-up-and-drop service segment remained a billion-dollar question.
