It was the evening of 25 March 2020 in Mumbai. Raj Mehta, who had recently completed his MBA degree, came across a news item from 2019 that said Jet Airways had suspended its operations because it could not pay its massive debt. Raj had recently started investing in stock markets. With this news, he was perplexed about the future of the companies he wanted to invest in. He wanted to find out if there was a way to predict bankruptcy.

He contacted his friend Abhi, who has been in the field of investments for the last four years. They met in a café near Raj’s house. Abhi told Raj, “I have heard of a model called Altman’s Z-score, but I have not used it. I will try to send you some material.”

He sent the following material to Raj:

Professor Edward Altman had created a model in 1968 using discriminant analysis for publicly traded large manufacturing firms. This model attempts to predict bankruptcy in the next two years. He later modified the model to suit private companies and service firms.

The equation below calculates the Z-score:

Z=1.2X1+1.4X2+3.3X3+0.6X4+1.0X5

The variables X1 through X5 are five ratios that can be computed using the financial statements of a firm.

X1=Net Working CapitalTotal Assets

X2=Retained EarningsTotal Assets

X3=Earnings before Interest and TaxesTotal Assets

X4=Market Value of EquityBook Value of Liabilities

X5=SalesTotal Assets

If the Z-score is above 2.675, the firm is safe. If the score is below 1.81, there is a very high possibility of the firm experiencing bankruptcy. A score between 1.81 and 2.675 may indicate a possibility of bankruptcy.

Along with this material, Abhi sent the Balance Sheet and Profit and Loss Statement of the recently published annual report of ACC Limited. (Exhibit 1 and 2). He also mentioned that the market capitalization of ACC Limited as of 31 December 2019 was INR 271,474.66 million.

Raj went through the material and the financial statements but could not calculate the Z-score. He thought of calling his cousin, Ravi Grover, who worked in an investment banking firm.Page 3
