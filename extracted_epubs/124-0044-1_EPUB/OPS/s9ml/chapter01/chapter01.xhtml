Graphart: An industrial, environmentally-responsible company

Graphart is an industrial company specializing in the manufacture and sale of home interior items. These items become particularly sought after during festive seasons as they enhance the warm, festive ambiance in homes. The company has a strong devotion to corporate social responsibility (CSR), evidenced by several years of steadfast policy development. Consequently, their choices of materials, suppliers, and partners are made with exceptional care. The company’s robust reputation in the market echoes its image of quality and customer proximity. Graphart is based in a southern region of France, situated in an industrial area comprised of other companies that share a similar commitment to responsible business practices.

The company is comprised of several departments: General Management, Human Resources, Finance and Accounting, Research and Development, Sales and Marketing, Purchasing, Production, and Warehousing. Each department is staffed with a manager and several employees.

Management controller: A tool for strategic decision-making

As a management controller for the company, you work within the Finance and Accounting department. Your primary responsibilities include sales forecasting, analyzing production variances, managing supply, planning internal processes, tracking and validating expense reports, monitoring budgets, calculating and monitoring production costs, determining sales prices, margin analysis, and other ad hoc tasks.

It is the final quarter of 2023. Preparations for 2024 have begun, focusing on budgeting, sales forecasting, purchasing raw materials, and production planning. Your manager has requested that you evaluate the performance of two of the company’s products, the Wood product and the Green product.

Following extensive research and interviews within the company, data was collected from both managers and employees. After transforming this data into a usable format, the performance of the company’s products was calculated and analyzed (Appendix 1). The meetings also provided additional valuable information for the analysis (Appendix 2).

Appendix 2. Management controller’s qualitative data

In discussions with both managers and employees, you uncover a problem in the production department. A machine integral to the manufacture of the Wood products is antiquated and requires regular repairs. Despite this, the director wishes to retain it due to a preference for traditional models, which are perceived as more durable and efficient.

In the supply department, there’s a problem with a key supplier. The supplier has raised prices to maintain the quality of raw materials 1 and 2. The director must decide whether to accept this change or search for a new supplier. A decision has not yet been made. Your analysis could potentially assist with the decision-making process.

The target analysis results were €3.05 for the Wood product and €4.95 for the Green product.
